# Development Diary - 26/10/2025 - 12/11/2025

## Task / Work Summary
During this period, I focused on securing access to suitable GPU computing resources required for the deepfake detection component of my project. The plan was to use the university’s Hadoop clusters to run pretrained AI models on full video datasets. However, this process was unexpectedly delayed due to the cluster still being under configuration.
The lack of a confirmed setup date created a major project blocker between 26/10/2025 and 12/11/2025, preventing any progress on GPU-dependent work. I spent this time following up on access, exploring alternative solutions such as AWS and the university’s supercomputer (Sciama), and reassessing my project timeline to manage the impact of the delay.
This task period primarily involved logistical coordination, risk assessment, and identifying feasible computational options to ensure the project’s technical goals could still be met without compromising quality or feasibility.

## Context / Problem / Challenge

A significant and unexpected blocker arose between 26/10/2025 and 12/11/2025 when I was attempting to secure GPU resources necessary for running pretrained deepfake detection models such as XceptionNet and EfficientNet.
Initially, I was advised to contact the department responsible for managing the university’s high-performance Hadoop clusters, as they were believed to include GPU-equipped nodes suitable for my project. However, I soon learned that these clusters were still in the configuration stage and would not be operational until at least late November or December.

This created a serious scheduling issue. The models I am using require GPU acceleration to process full video datasets — CPU-based execution would take days per analysis instead of minutes. I had allocated this period (late October to mid-November) for deepfake model integration and testing, but without confirmed access to GPU hardware, progress came to a complete standstill.

Despite two rounds of correspondence and follow-ups, I was unable to obtain a definitive configuration date. This uncertainty significantly increased my anxiety about meeting project milestones, as this was not a minor delay but a core dependency. I had built my timeline assuming that GPU access — a resource other students had previously used — would be available through the department. The lack of clarity left me blindsided and unsure how to proceed.

## Approach / Method

To mitigate the risk of further delay, I explored alternative computing options. I investigated the use of AWS SageMaker and other cloud GPU instances, but the projected cost was over £2000, which was not financially viable. This option was therefore dismissed as unsustainable.

Given that the university cluster was not available and commercial cloud alternatives were cost-prohibitive, I began considering alternative institutional resources. I pivoted my strategy and reached out to another faculty member to inquire about access to the university’s supercomputer (Sciama). My goal was to determine whether Sciama could provide an accessible, GPU-backed environment for executing my deepfake analysis workloads. This proactive step was essential to avoid compromising the project’s computational integrity.

## Response / Solution

Although this situation has not yet been fully resolved, taking early action prevented total stagnation. By initiating contact regarding Sciama, I created a possible route forward that aligns with the technical needs of the project. This pivot ensures that I may still be able to complete deepfake model training and analysis without having to simplify or reduce the project scope.

The impact of this delay, however, has been substantial. The waiting period between 26/10/2025 and 12/11/2025 — during which no GPU resources were available — resulted in approximately three weeks of lost development time. Tasks dependent on GPU processing, such as full video analysis testing, were postponed. Consequently, I am now behind schedule and must condense later development stages, increasing the workload and stress in the final phase of the project.

While the experience has been stressful and disruptive, it has also reinforced the importance of risk management and resource contingency planning when dealing with hardware-dependent AI projects. I plan to adjust my Gantt chart to reflect this delay and continue working toward accessing alternative computational resources to maintain the project’s integrity.
